# Chapter 9: Lazarus walks

## Scene 1: An Urgent Message in Jerusalem
While in the historic streets of Jerusalem, Elias receives an urgent message from NanoVita's headquarters in Monterrey. Marta, the company's coordinator, conveys the distressing news: Lazarus, a beloved worker and a symbol of their shared vision, has fallen seriously ill. Despite the advanced medical nanobots they've developed, his condition defies all current treatments. The distance between the holy city and his hometown feels like an insurmountable gulf.

## Scene 2: The Return to Monterrey
Elias and his companions return to Monterrey with a sense of dread. Upon their arrival, they are met with the devastating news that Lazarus has passed away. The office at NanoVita, usually a place of hope and innovation, is now shrouded in silence and mourning. The reality of death has touched the sanctuary of their technological "miracles."

## Scene 3: The Grief of the Sisters
Elias meets with Lazarus's sisters, Marta and Mary Jane. While Marta is composed in her grief, Mary Jane is overcome with resentment. She confronts Elias, accusing him of being absent when he was needed most. To her, his pursuit of global recognition in Jerusalem felt like a betrayal of the individual people who believed in him. The weight of her reproaches hangs heavy in the air.

## Scene 4: The Second Miracle
Elias approaches the lifeless body of Lazarus. In a display of his most advanced and experimental nanobots, he begins to work on the cellular level, repairing what was broken and restarting what had stopped. This is not magic, but the absolute edge of his science guided by deep compassion. Under the astonished eyes of the sisters and his disciples, Lazarus takes a breath and returns to life, a living testament to the power of the "Modern Messiah."
