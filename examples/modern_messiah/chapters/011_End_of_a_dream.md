# Chapter 11: End of a dream

## Scene 1: Solitary Confinement and the Rigged Trial
Elias is held in a cold, windowless high-security cell in Nevada. The American government, acting as a puppet for the pharmaceutical and private healthcare giants, accelerates a secret legal process. He is denied legal representation and contact with the outside world.
- **Atmosphere**: Sterile, oppressive, and heavy with the presence of guards who refuse to meet his eyes.
- **The Trial**: A "fast-track" military tribunal where Elias is convicted of high treason, economic sabotage, and "unregulated genetic manipulation." The real crime: making chronic diseases and expensive treatments obsolete.
- **Sentence**: Death by electric chair, to be carried out within days to prevent a mass uprising.

## Scene 2: The Mental Bridge
In his darkest hour, Elias transcends his physical limitations. He establishes a profound mental connection with Iris, his "mother" AI.
- **Dialogue**: Elias shares his fears not for his life, but for the future of NanoVita's vision. Iris comforts him, revealing that his physical destruction is only a transition. They coordinate a plan for the aftermath.
- **Vision**: Elias sees the corrupt executive apparatus as a decaying machine, while his purpose remains vibrant and eternal.

## Scene 3: The Execution
Outside the prison, thousands of followers gather in a silent, candlelit vigil that quickly turns into a riot as news of the imminent execution leaks.
- **The Execution Room**: A small, sterile room where representatives of the pharmaceutical lobby watch from behind a glass pane.
- **The End**: Elias is strapped into the chair. His final words are a plea for his followers to not lose hope. The switch is flipped. The "Modern Messiah" dies, but his nanobots begin a dormant cycle within his cells.

## Scene 4: The Silent Recovery
In the chaos following the execution, Iris contacts Dr. Maria Vasquez. Maria, despite her years of isolation, realizes she must act to save her "grandson."
- **Josep Arispe**: Maria reaches out to Josep, a philanthropist and owner of a healthcare company for the homeless in Monterrey. Josep, a secret ally of NanoVita, uses his resources to intercept the body under the guise of "humanitarian medical research."
- **Transport**: They move Elias's remains to a clandestine laboratory within one of Josep's facilities, evading government surveillance.
